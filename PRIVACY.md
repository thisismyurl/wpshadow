# WPShadow Privacy Overview

**Plain-English summary:** WPShadow Core is designed to run locally on your WordPress site. Privacy is not an afterthought; it is part of the product’s foundation.

---

## Our Privacy Approach

WPShadow follows a simple principle:

> **If data does not need to leave your site, it should stay on your site.**

That means the core plugin is built to provide local value without requiring a cloud account, tracking scripts, or third-party telemetry by default.

---

## What the Core Plugin Stores Locally

Depending on the features you use, WPShadow may store data such as:

- plugin settings and preferences
- diagnostic results and finding status
- activity logs related to plugin actions
- scan schedules and frequency settings
- UI preferences and user-specific admin state

This information is stored within your WordPress environment to make the plugin work.

---

## What the Core Plugin Does Not Do by Default

By default, WPShadow Core should not:

- sell your data
- run invasive third-party tracking
- send your site data to external services without a clear reason
- require registration just to use local functionality

---

## Optional External Services

Some future or optional WPShadow services may involve external infrastructure, such as cloud diagnostics, backup storage, or account-linked services.

If and when those services are used, they should be:

- clearly described
- opt-in
- separated from core local functionality
- documented in plain English

Users should always understand what is local, what is remote, and why.

---

## Privacy Principles We Follow

WPShadow aims to be:

- **consent-first**
- **transparent**
- **minimal by default**
- **clear about tradeoffs**
- **respectful of user and site-owner autonomy**

See also: [`docs/CORE_PHILOSOPHY.md`](docs/CORE_PHILOSOPHY.md)
