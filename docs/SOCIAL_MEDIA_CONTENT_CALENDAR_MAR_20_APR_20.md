# 7. Calendar Dates Clinic — Scheduled Posts, Timezones, and Related Issues

## Facebook
**Calendar Dates Clinic:** When a post publishes at the wrong time

A lot of scheduling problems are really timezone problems.

If a post goes live too early, too late, or seems to miss its slot, start with these checks:
- your WordPress timezone setting
- your scheduler or plugin timezone
- whether your expected publish time matches your site’s local time
- whether your scheduled tasks are running as expected

Why this matters:
time-based issues can look confusing, but they are often fixable once you know where the clock settings live.

A calm first step:
check your WordPress timezone and compare it with the time you expected.

# Reddit Discussion Ideas to Look For

## Threads to watch for
- **“My scheduled post missed its publish time”**
  - Offer a calm checklist: timezone, scheduler settings, cron or scheduled task behavior, caching delays.
- **“What plugin should I use to monitor my site?”**
  - Lead with what to evaluate first: clarity, local vs cloud checks, backups, rollback, readable alerts.
- **“How do I know if my backups actually work?”**
  - Share the restore-test mindset, staging or local copy, and what to verify after restore.
- **“WordPress warnings make no sense”**
  - Offer plain-language translation of common warning types and what to check first.
- **“How do I make site maintenance less overwhelming?”**
  - Suggest a weekly 10-minute routine: backups, updates, comments or spam review, one small improvement.
- **“Accessibility quick wins for a small site?”**
  - Share one-check, one-fix advice: headings, alt text, contrast, form labels.
- **“What does local monitoring vs cloud monitoring mean?”**
  - Good moment to explain:
    - **WPShadow Guardian** = local, inside WPShadow Core
    - **Cloud Guardian diagnostics** = part of planned WPShadow Cloud
- **“How should I document fixes for clients or teammates?”**
  - Connect to open formats, checklists, and sanitized “how we fixed it” notes.
- **“What’s in your weekly site care routine?”**
  - Great for helpful, non-promotional participation.
- **“Beginner-friendly alternatives to scary security plugins?”**
  - Emphasize explanation, safe defaults, backups, and clarity over alarm language.
