# Support WPShadow

If WPShadow has helped you, there are several meaningful ways to support the project.

This document exists to keep that support request aligned with the project’s core philosophy:

- **advice, not pressure**
- **free as possible**
- **community before conversion**
- **clear value before any ask**

---

## The Most Helpful Ways to Support the Project

### 1. Use the Plugin and Share Feedback
Real-world usage helps more than almost anything else.

Please tell us:
- what helped
- what confused you
- what felt trustworthy
- what still needs work

### 2. Leave a Review on WordPress.org
Honest reviews help other site owners discover the project and decide whether it is worth trying.

### 3. Star and Share the Repository
A GitHub star, link, or mention helps improve discoverability and community reach.

### 4. Contribute Documentation, Bug Reports, or Fixes
Clear docs and thoughtful bug reports are some of the most valuable contributions a project like this can receive.

See: [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](CONTRIBUTING.md)

### 5. Sponsor the Work
If GitHub Sponsors is enabled for the project, the **Sponsor** button on GitHub is the preferred way to contribute financially.

If additional support options are introduced later, they should always be explained transparently and without pressure.

### 6. Choose a Sponsorship Level

WP Shadow uses a tiered sponsorship model so people can support the project at a level that fits their needs and budget.

Current monthly sponsorship levels:

- **$10 - Supporter**: name on the sponsor thank-you page if desired, monthly progress update, sponsor badge or supporter role
- **$25 - Backer**: sponsor-only discussion space, voting in roadmap polls, access to behind-the-scenes development notes
- **$50 - Advocate**: preview of upcoming releases, quarterly live Q&A or office hours, early insight into feature direction
- **$100 - Champion**: priority triage on bug reports, one light annual review of a site setup or use case, recognition as a core community sponsor
- **$250 - Professional Sponsor**: one 30-minute sponsor session per month, priority support queue, early access to beta workflow changes, recognition as a professional sponsor
- **$500 - Team Sponsor**: up to 1 hour of direct support or consultation per month, private email contact path, name or logo in sponsor acknowledgments
- **$1,000 - Growth Sponsor**: quarterly planning call, early access to larger features or workflow changes, priority response window for sponsor questions, up to 2 hours of direct support per month
- **$2,500 - Platform Sponsor**: monthly strategy call, prioritized investigation of issues affecting a sponsor environment, small sponsor-requested improvements where they fit the product and can ship upstream, recognition tied to regional WordCamp participation where appropriate, up to 4 hours of direct collaboration per month
- **$5,000 - Strategic Sponsor**: private Slack, Discord, or email channel, direct roadmap input, architecture and deployment guidance, sponsor-priority support and implementation guidance, recognition tied to global WordCamp participation where appropriate, up to 8 hours of reserved collaboration per month
- **$10,000 - Founding Partner**: named seat on a sponsor advisory council, monthly 1:1 planning call, early input on major architecture decisions, dedicated private communication channel, priority response target during business hours, custom diagnostics or treatments subject to scope agreement, principal sponsor recognition, and up to 12 to 16 hours of reserved collaboration per month under a separate partnership agreement

High-touch sponsorship levels are subject to availability and scope. Sponsorship supports the project and may include collaboration time, but it does not buy exclusive ownership of the plugin or paywalled access to core features.

## What Support Helps Fund

Support makes it easier to invest time in:

- accessibility and usability improvements
- bug fixing and maintenance
- clearer documentation and knowledge base content
- safer diagnostics and treatments
- community support and onboarding
- release quality and testing

---

## Our Promise

Financial support should never turn WPShadow into nagware.

We do **not** want support asks to feel like:
- guilt
- fear
- fake urgency
- pay-to-understand-your-own-site behavior

If the project asks for support, it should be because the work has already been helpful and you freely want to keep it going.

## Thank You

Whether you support WPShadow with time, feedback, reviews, code, or money: thank you.

That support helps keep the project useful, humane, and sustainable.
